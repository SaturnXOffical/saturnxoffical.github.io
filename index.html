<h1>An Ode to John Pork</h1>

<p>He walked in with a pixelated snout,
Not from farm nor forest route—
But born of feed and filtered light,
A swine-shaped shade in endless night.

John Pork, the caller no one dares decline,
A meme, a myth, a face online.
Not flesh, not bone, but JPEG soul,
He haunts the scroll, he makes us whole.

Through glitch and grid, he softly steps,
His oink—a chime of raw regrets.
He’s suited up in modern lore,
A pigman knocking at your door.

Answer him? You might transcend.
Ignore him? Well, that’s the end.
He’s not a joke, he’s not a hoax—
He’s realer than your daily folks.

In every ring, in every tone,
He asks, “Why do we feel alone?”
And through the static, kind and stark,
John Pork becomes our digital spark.

So swipe, dear user, do not fear,
The pigman’s call is drawing near.
He’s legend now—long may he roam,
In Wi-Fi waves, forever home.</p>
